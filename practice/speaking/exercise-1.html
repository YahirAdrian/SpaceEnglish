Speaking practice 1: A stressful job

David knows that his job is damaging his health. Luckily, Sophie is there to listen and offer advice.

Start with the preparation task. Then watch the video and follow the instructions to practice your speaking.

It's your turn to practice

It is time for you to practice.

Transcript

Sophie: Hi, David. You look tired.

David: Hi Sophie. Mmm, I did not sleep.

Sophie: Are you okay? What’s the matter? 

David: Well, as you know, I recently completed my studies and now I work at a hospital. The long work hours make me very tired. I have many duties and sleepless nights have become typical since I started attending to sick people.

Sophie: Being a nurse during residency must be extremely hard.

David: Yes, it is very hard. I work long hours. There are emergencies and I don't have time to eat or rest. 

Sophie: That sounds difficult. What helps you keep trying when the work is hard? 

David: To tell the truth, the hard things don't ever fully stop. However, helping people get healthy again inspires me to keep going.

Sophie: How can you stay positive?

David: It's hard but talking to my colleagues helps.

Sophie: You need to take care of yourself, David. Getting sick from stress will not help anyone. Have you thought about ways to relax? 

David: Honestly, I have no time.

Sophie: You need balance in your life, David. Maybe doing relaxing activities would help, even just taking some minutes to clear your mind or do something fun.

David: You are right. I will try to rest more. And do some fun things too. I need a balance between work and rest. Thank you for the talk, Sophie.
